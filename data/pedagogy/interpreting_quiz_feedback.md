# How to Interpret Quiz Feedback

Quizzes are not just grades — they are diagnostic signals. How you read a score,
and especially a *trend* of scores, determines whether the next attempt improves.

## Read the trend, not just the number

- A **rising** trend across recent attempts means your current strategy works;
  keep going and gradually increase difficulty.
- A **flat** trend suggests you have plateaued. Change *how* you practise: move
  from recognition (multiple choice review) to recall (closed-book self-testing).
- A **declining** trend is an early-warning sign. It usually reflects growing gaps
  in prerequisites or fading engagement, not sudden loss of ability.

## Turn feedback into action

1. Categorise each missed question: careless slip, misunderstood concept, or
   never studied. Each category needs a different fix.
2. Re-attempt only after you have actively reviewed the underlying concept, not
   immediately. Spacing the retry strengthens memory.
3. Track which topics repeat as errors; those are where to spend the next session.

## Reframe failure

A low score on a low-stakes quiz is cheap information. Students who treat quizzes
as practice — expecting and analysing mistakes — outperform those who avoid them
to protect their confidence.
