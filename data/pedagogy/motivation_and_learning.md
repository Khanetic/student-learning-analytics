# Motivation and Learning

Motivation is not a fixed trait you either have or lack; it is a state that can be
engineered through how you set goals, interpret setbacks, and connect work to what
you value.

## Three levers of motivation

- **Autonomy:** choice fuels effort. Even small choices — which problem set to do
  first, which example to study — increase ownership and persistence.
- **Competence:** motivation grows when tasks are challenging *but achievable*.
  Break work into steps where success is likely, then raise the bar gradually.
- **Relatedness:** connecting with peers, tutors, or a study group sustains effort
  during hard stretches and reduces the isolation common in distance learning.

## Mindset and setbacks

A **growth mindset** — viewing ability as improvable through effort and strategy —
helps students treat a poor quiz as feedback rather than a verdict. When you
notice "I can't do this," add the word *yet*, then identify one concrete next step.

## Protecting motivation when at risk

Disengaged students benefit most from quick, visible wins. Complete one small,
meaningful task today to rebuild momentum, and reconnect the course to a personal
goal — a career, a project, a question you care about — to renew its purpose.
