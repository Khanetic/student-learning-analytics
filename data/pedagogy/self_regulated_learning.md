# Self-Regulated Learning Strategies

Self-regulated learning (SRL) describes how successful students actively plan,
monitor, and adjust their own learning rather than studying passively. Research
by Zimmerman and others frames SRL as a cycle of three phases.

## The SRL cycle

1. **Forethought** — set a specific, achievable goal for each study session and
   decide which strategy to use. "I will work two practice quizzes on chapter 3"
   beats "I will study some."
2. **Performance** — study while monitoring your own understanding. Pause and ask
   "Could I explain this to someone else?" If not, slow down or switch tactics.
3. **Self-reflection** — after studying, compare what you achieved against your
   goal and decide what to change next time.

## Practical tactics

- **Goal setting:** break a large course into weekly sub-goals tied to concrete
  deliverables (a quiz attempt, a summary, three solved problems).
- **Self-monitoring:** keep a short log of what you studied and how confident you
  felt. Patterns reveal which topics need another pass.
- **Strategy adjustment:** if re-reading is not working, switch to active recall
  or practice testing. Effort is necessary, but *directed* effort matters more.

## Why it helps disengaged learners

Students whose engagement is slipping often lack structure rather than ability.
A single weekly goal plus a five-minute reflection restores a sense of control,
which is one of the strongest predictors of persistence in online courses.
