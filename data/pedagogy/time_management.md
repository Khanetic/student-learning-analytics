# Time Management for Students

Distance and online students rarely fail for lack of intelligence; they fall
behind because unstructured time quietly disappears. Managing *when* and *how
long* you study is often more decisive than the total hours invested.

## Build a regular rhythm

- **Fixed study blocks:** schedule recurring sessions at the same times each week.
  Regularity lowers the activation cost of starting and builds a habit loop.
- **Short and frequent beats long and rare:** four 45-minute sessions across a
  week produce better retention than one four-hour cram, thanks to spaced
  practice and reduced fatigue.
- **Protect the first 10 minutes:** begin with the smallest possible task to
  overcome inertia, then let momentum carry you.

## Manage the session

- Use a timer (for example 25 minutes focused, 5 minutes rest) to keep attention
  fresh and make progress visible.
- Remove one distraction before you start rather than relying on willpower during
  the session.
- End each block by writing the *first step* of the next one, so restarting is
  effortless.

## When you have fallen behind

Triage rather than panic: list outstanding tasks, rank by deadline and weight,
and complete the highest-impact item first. Submitting an imperfect assignment on
time usually beats a perfect one submitted late.
